My idea (let's call it Up-Gadget) was to create a web-app that could scan given appliance or gadget or/and take in given specs, and provide a visual breakdown of each appliance/gadget, and how it could be upgraded. For example, given an iPhone 16 256 GB Storage, etc., the web-app could scan and provide a visual breakdown of the parts. Once scanned or details typed in, it will start by giving the user an overview of upgradeable parts through an API or otherwise. Afterwards, it will be giving the user a checklist of what tool(s) will be required to open up the phone, in able to take that part out, as well as various options through an built-in community marketplace where people sell technology parts, or from popular websites such as amazon, etc. Once the user has the tools, the web-app could provide guidance through an AI feature on how to properly take apart the phone through accessing the camera, afte which the user can take a picture and post the part to sell in the built in marketplace, or if the user chooses not to sell, provide great options for recycling the part(s). After the upgrade is complete, the user could update the profile of his phone on the app, so that the app saves the upgraded profile of the phone for possible future upgradeability.
